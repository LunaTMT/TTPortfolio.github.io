Concepts used during assignment

Modeling a problem

  • Decomposition is the process of breaking down a problem into several components.

  • Abstraction is the process of hiding all but relevant data to reduce the complexity of a situation.

States

  • Initial state – Where you are and what you have at the start.

  • Goal state – The point you would like to reach (normally a solution).

  • Terminal state – Reaching a dead end, there are no other paths you can traverse to attain a solution.

Constraints

  • Real - a real impediment that hinders your ability to reach a goal.

  • Imaginary – a constructed non-existent impediment that hinders your ability to reach a goal.

  • Physical – a impediment on the human body that hinders your ability to reach a goal.

Memory

  • Short term – also known as working memory, is a form of memory in which small amounts of information can be stored at any given time for a brief period of time. Miller’s law tries to quantify this using the 7±2 law.

  • Long term – ‘infinite capacity, different forms of LTM with varying strength. Two major subdivisions are explicit and implicit memory.

    Explicit memories are those that you consciously remember such as an event in your life or a particular fact.

    Implicit memories are those that you do without thinking about, like riding a bike – you once learned how, and you remembered how, but now do it without conscious thought.’

  • Millers law – Miller had proposed a rule to attempt to quantify human STM limitations - The law states that a normal human can deal with 7±2 chunks of information at any given time.

Search methods

  • Depth First Search – An algorithm for traversing or searching a tree starting at the root nodes and exploring as far as possible across each branch. This could be put into perspective when finding a problem by viewing all your options and to explore all possible options.

  • Breadth first search An algorithm for traversing or searching a tree starting at the root nodes and exploring each neighbor below it. This would be the same as viewing all possible options from the base of a problem then check all possible outcomes and selectively choosing the best course of action to take next.

Conceptions

  • Initial – Your first idea.

  • Pre-conceived – forming an idea without having sufficient evidence for its truth or usefulness.

Validation

  • Validation – Throughout the assignment it had been extremely important for us as a group to continuously have our work validated by our lecturer to ensure we were on the correct path.

Conceptions

  • Simons Model (functional model of problem solving) Structured approach to solving a problem
    - detect
    - Decide
    - Remember
    - Act

  • Von Neumann model – computer architecture based on the stored program concept in which instruction data and program data are stored in the same memory.

  • Turing machine - Hypothetical mathematical model of a computer system with a predefined set of rules to determine a result from a set of any input. This was first proposed by Alan Turing and can model any machine.

The Boston dice game

The following assignment is an individual project to create a program that will play a variation of the Boston dice game.

The Boston dice game:

- Each player has three throws of the dice
- These values are totaled
- Whoever has the largest total wins that round
- There are five rounds

Based on my program there are two possible ways to win, which follows:

- Wining the most rounds in one game
- having the greatest total overall

There is also a player vs computer variant to which the second player being the computer does not have to press enter.
